Augmenting Image LLMs for Diverse Video Grounding Tasks Without Training

Code

Abstract

Video Large Language Models (Video LLMs) demonstrate strong performance in captioning and question answering but struggle to accurately ground text queries in videos. Existing methods often depend on temporal-aware training and extensive instruction tuning, which are costly and limit generalization. We propose a training-free framework that adapts Image LLMs for video grounding through a two-stage pipeline. First, Query-Guided Multi-Granularity Image Description uses an Image LLM to convert video frames into structured descriptions capturing scene- and object-level semantics. Second, Guided Grounding Prediction employs a tailored prompt template that integrates these descriptions with task-specific instructions, enabling an LLM to reason over temporal context and localize moments. Extensive experiments on three grounding tasks across diverse domains demonstrate that our approach surpasses several Video LLMs and achieves results competitive with specialized training-based models, highlighting its effectiveness, adaptability, and strong generalization.

Paradigm comparison for video grounding.
